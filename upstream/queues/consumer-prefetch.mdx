🐇 **Upstream reference:** [Consumer Prefetch](https://www.rabbitmq.com/docs/consumer-prefetch)

Prefetch (QoS — Quality of Service) limits the number of unacknowledged messages the broker delivers to a consumer before waiting for acknowledgements. It is the primary flow-control mechanism between broker and consumer.

## Setting Prefetch

```python
# Per-consumer prefetch (recommended)
channel.basic_qos(prefetch_count=10, global_qos=False)

# Channel-wide shared prefetch (less common)
channel.basic_qos(prefetch_count=50, global_qos=True)
```

- `prefetch_count = 0` — unlimited (default). The broker pushes messages as fast as the consumer can receive. Dangerous with slow consumers.
- `global_qos = False` — limit applies per consumer on this channel (recommended)
- `global_qos = True` — limit shared across all consumers on this channel

> For quorum queues: `global_qos=True` is not supported. Use per-consumer prefetch only.

## How Prefetch Affects Throughput

Prefetch creates a pipeline: the broker sends N messages before waiting for acks. While the consumer is processing message 1, messages 2–N are already in transit. This hides round-trip latency and maximizes consumer throughput.

**Too low:** Consumer is idle waiting for the next message after each ack. Throughput is limited by round-trip latency.

**Too high:** A slow or crashing consumer holds many messages in memory/flight that can't be redelivered to other consumers.

**Rule of thumb:** Start at `prefetch = 2 × (processing time / round-trip time)`. For most LAN deployments with sub-millisecond round-trips and 10ms processing time, prefetch = 10–50 is a reasonable range.

## Prefetch and Multiple Consumers

With per-consumer prefetch and multiple consumers on the same queue:
- Each consumer has its own in-flight window
- A slow consumer only blocks its own window — other consumers receive their messages
- Total in-flight messages across all consumers = `prefetch_count × consumer_count`

## Prefetch and Work Distribution

Low prefetch (1–2) results in round-robin-like distribution. High prefetch can cause uneven distribution — a fast consumer can hold all in-flight messages, leaving slow consumers idle.

For equal distribution with variable processing times, prefetch=1 is the most fair but lowest throughput. Tune based on whether fairness or throughput is more important.

## AceMQ Take

**Never leave prefetch at 0 in production.** Unlimited prefetch causes a fast broker to overwhelm a slow consumer, buffering thousands of messages in the consumer's memory before any acks arrive. On consumer restart, all those buffered (but unacked) messages must be redelivered — causing a burst to other consumers.

**Prefetch = concurrency.** If your consumer processes messages concurrently (thread pool, goroutine pool, async tasks), prefetch should match your concurrency level. A single-threaded consumer with prefetch=100 means 99 messages sit in a buffer doing nothing.

**Measure, don't guess.** Instrument consumer ack latency and queue depth. If queue depth is non-zero and consumer ack rate is lower than publish rate, increase concurrency or prefetch. If consumer memory is high, decrease prefetch.
